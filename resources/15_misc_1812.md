Kentish Weekly Post or Canterbury Journal - Friday 12 June 1812

Manchester Rioters.—The eight following were left for execution on Saturday next, viz.

James Smith, Thomas Kerfoot, Job Fletcher, Abraham Charlson, John Howarth, John Lee, Thomas Hoyle, and Hannah Smith.

The sentence of the other principal rioters were as follow:—

John Hurst (the notorious General Ludd), Christopher Medcalf, James Brierley, Henry Thwaite, Joseph Greenhalgh, Samuel Ratcliffe, Thomas Pickup. John Burney, James Knowles, John Fisher, and Thomas Holden, were severally sentenced to be transported for the term of seven years.

John Hope and Samuel Crossley, for rioting and stealing at Worsley, were also sentenced to seven years transportation.
